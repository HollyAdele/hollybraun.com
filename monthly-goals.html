Monthly Goals

I have more interests than time. But I have finally found a way to pursue hobbies while being a stay-at-home mom of three.

Before I discovered this, I didn't accomplish much with my free time. Unless you call being completely up-to-date with my news feed an accomplishment. Which I don't.

If I found myself with some free time, I would just turn to Facebook. I had a thousand things I wanted to do, and a thousand more that I wanted me to want to do. But just deciding which task to tackle took up most of my free time. If I happened to work on it a bit, I used up the rest of the time just getting set up and reorienting myself.

These days, I have something better. Every few months, I make a list of things I want to learn about or work on. Then I assign them to coming months, and just do one thing a month. It's awesome. I get to focus completely on one subject at a time. I don't spend so much time trying to remember what I did last time. Also, I don't feel guilty pursuing a personal interest (like scrapbooking) instead of a duty (like researching my family history) because I know I will get to family history in just a few months.

Bryan used to do stuff like this before we were married, and he made a career with what he started during one of his monthly goals. This guy also talked about it at TED.

The latter allows me to make meals for pennies. I have decided to bake bread at home, and I am contemplating making cheese and yogurt at home as well. Heidi loves baking. It's a fun activity to do with kids that saves you money.
